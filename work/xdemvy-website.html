XDEMVY Website — Case Study

Encouraging disease awareness through usability

A full UX/UI redesign that coincided with a brand refresh — strengthening usability, accessibility, and engagement across the site.

RoleGraphic Designer
ToolsFigma, Illustrator, Photoshop
DurationQ3 2025
CollaborationDirected by Group Art Supervisor

Overview

Working under the direction of the Group Art Supervisor, I was responsible for translating existing research findings into UX and UI improvements across the site. The redesign coincided with a full brand refresh and was informed by UX research provided by the client — together, these inputs created an opportunity to strengthen usability, accessibility, and overall engagement across the site.

  • Provide concise website overviews with easy access to details
  • Reduce cognitive load and guide users to relevant information faster
  • Create a guided experience that turns engagement into action

The challenge

Poorly structured content and distracting calls to action led users to drop off. Addressing these issues was key to enhancing usability and enabling focused information retention.

Business need: create straightforward user journeys that allow clear access to information.
User need: simplify distracting content and develop clear pathways.

How might we build clear user journeys, so users can quickly take in what they're learning and know what steps to take next?

The process

Using UX research that had been outsourced, I laid the foundation of the website by creating wireframes — structurally laying out the information with a focus on hierarchy and usability. We then developed a design system incorporating the brand refresh, ensuring cohesion across 100+ pages of the website, desktop and mobile combined.

Elevating patient testimonials

Existing testimonial content felt visually flat and easy to overlook, limiting emotional engagement. I explored layout options that gave newly recorded patient videos greater prominence and visual weight.

  • Increased prominence of new patient videos for more stopping power over the previous layout
  • Placed the quote directly next to the video for the first six, allowing users to quickly scan through information
  • Used rounded rectangles over organic shapes — these scale cleanly across breakpoints and reinforce the tone of the new brand
  • Anchored the photo top-left through a hover effect, so users stay grounded in who's speaking as new content is revealed, building trust
  • Placed a "Watch Now" button above the name on hover, surfacing the video at the peak moment of interest

The final solution elevated select videos into a more immersive format, while supporting testimonials remained in a structured card layout for scanability.

Simplifying resources & downloads

The Resources & Downloads page contained valuable information but experienced quick drop-offs. Dense layouts and competing options made it difficult to scan or prioritize content.

  • Reorganized content architecture with condensed cards to promote scannability and strengthen hierarchy
  • Created a dropdown structure for various languages, progressively revealing content so users can explore at their own pace
  • Updated language to clearly state the options users can choose from when accessing assets

These choices reduced cognitive load while preserving access to detailed materials.

ISI expand / collapse

The ISI section needed to remain visible for compliance, but previously occupied a large portion of the viewport, interrupting content flow. I proposed an expandable/collapsible pattern that preserved visibility while allowing users to control how much space it occupied. Typography and spacing were refined to reduce its visual dominance without compromising legibility.

This approach balanced regulatory requirements with usability, improving content flow while maintaining compliance.

Outcomes

While full performance metrics were not available, the redesign directly addressed friction points identified in research and created clearer pathways for users navigating health-related decisions.

  • Reduced visual clutter across key pages
  • Clarified CTA hierarchy, particularly after quiz completion
  • Elevated patient stories to create stronger emotional engagement
  • Streamlined dense informational sections without sacrificing access
